The idea of restricted A'-probing for the closest DP was originally proposed for `absolutive-only' extraction cases in Ergative-Absolutive languages (syntactic ergativity) by Aldridge(2004, 2008, 2017), and other subsequent studies. However, recently, the same extraction restrictions have been argued to be present in non-ergative languages such as Turkish and Rejang (see Branan & Erlewine 2024, and some references therein). In other words, the phenomenon of A'-probing for the closest DP is not unique to only ergative languages. It seems to be more general than earlier thought.

In this study, I present novel data from two Mabia (formerly, Gur) languages, <b>Kusaal</b> and <b>Gurene</b> (spoken mainly in Northern Ghana), that exhibit an extraction asymmetry between local subject focus (LSF henceforth) on the one hand, and non-subject focus (both local & non-local) (NSF henceforth) plus non-local subject focus (NLSF henceforth) on the other. I argue that (a) such extraction asymmetry can be attributed to restricted A'-probing for the closest DP, and that (b) the extraction asymmetry is reflected by a distinction in morphological focus marking. I further consider three alternative approaches, and argue that only one out of these alternative approaches looks promising. The other two are faced with some challenges, which I will discuss.
