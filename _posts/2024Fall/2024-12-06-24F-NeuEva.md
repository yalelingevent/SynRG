Ever since the unaccusativity hypothesis was proposed, it has been noted that some intransitives allow for both an unergative and an unaccusative use, with systematic differences in interpretation. The unergative/unaccusative alternation is peculiar in that neither variant is semantically or syntactically a subset of the other, contrasting in this respect with, for instance, the causative-inchoative alternation. In this talk, I argue that variable unaccusativity is just one instance of a broader class of argument-structural alternations with the same profile, which I label variable linking alternations. I provide a cross-linguistic survey of different cases of variable linking, highlight the methodological implications of variable linking for argument structure research, and discuss the theoretical issues it raises.
